About Me

After completing both of my degrees in Music, I decided to take a Sales Associate position at Atlanta's largest retail music store in order to get a first hand look at the music industry. After realizing that I didn't want to work in the music industry and that I just wanted to perform, I decided to start working at Georgia State University as an Academic Advisor. I felt that this position would allow me the opportunity to really help students and directly impact their success. I loved helping the students but found that I was unable to exercise my creative problem solving skills. This is when I discovered computer programming.

While exploring the internet one night, I discovered Free Code Camp, a free resource for learning computer programming. I decided to try giving it a shot and registered for the website. After starting, I just couldn't stop! I found myself studying for hours on end during all of my free time (and sneaking some studying in at work). After self studying for nearly five months, I decided to take my skills to the next level and enroll in DigitalCrafts, a coding bootcamp where I am studying Full Stack development.

I believe that my skills as a jazz musician and my desire to work with others to help people make me an excellent fit for any team!

Projects

MealSleuth

MealSleuth is a web application that allows the user to use their current location to find the nearest restaraunt that meets their cuisine, price point and distance. I used the Google Maps and Google Places API to gather information about each potential restaraunt and then used Javascript to populate the new information into the DOM.

Basic War!

Basic War is a simplified version of the popular card game, "War." I created this game using Python as the programming language and Pygame in order to display the content. I utilized elements of Object Oriented Programming to create a class that would essentially house all of my text for the different points throughout the game.
